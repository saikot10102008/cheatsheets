# Use of been, have been, has been, had been :
These words are all forms of the verb **be**, used to talk about states of being or actions that happen over time. The main difference lies in *when* the action happens and *who* is doing it.

---

## 1. Been (The Lone Participle)

**Been** is the past participle of "be." It **cannot** stand alone as the main verb of a sentence; it always needs a helper verb before it, like *have, has, had,* or *was/were* (in passive voice).

* **Correct:** I have **been** busy.
* **Incorrect:** I ~~been~~ busy.

---

## 2. Have Been vs. Has Been (Present Perfect)

We use **have been** and **has been** to talk about an action or state that started in the past and is **still true right now**, or just recently finished.

The choice between them depends entirely on the subject:

| Verb | Used With | Examples |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Have been** | I, You, We, They, and plural nouns | I **have been** a student here for three years.<br>

<br>They **have been** playing football since morning. |
| **Has been** | He, She, It, and singular nouns | She **has been** sick since Monday.<br>

<br>The weather **has been** wonderful today. |

---

## 3. Had Been (Past Perfect)

**Had been** is used to talk about an action or state that started in the past and **ended in the past** before another action happened.

Just like regular *had*, it is universal—it stays the same no matter what the subject is.

* **Example:** I **had been** a teacher for five years before I changed my career.
*(First I was a teacher, then I changed careers. Both happened in the past).*
* **Example:** The ground was wet because it **had been** raining all night.

---

## The Ultimate Summary Formula

To use these in sentences where an action is ongoing (Continuous Tense), use this structure:

$$[Subject] + [have/has/had] + been + [Verb + ing]$$

* **Present Continuous (Still happening):** "They **have been** *studying* for two hours." (They are still studying right now).
* **Past Continuous (Finished in the past):** "They **had been** *studying* for two hours before the power went out." (The studying stopped when the power cut out).
