"The brave" is typically **plural**.

When you use "the" followed by an adjective (like *the brave*, *the rich*, *the poor*, or *the young*), it functions as a collective noun meaning "people who are brave."

### Examples:

* "None but **the brave** deserve the fair." (Plural)
* "The home of **the brave**." (Plural)

Because it represents a group of people, it takes a **plural verb**:

* "The brave **are** remembered." (Not *"the brave is"*).

---

### The Exception

The only time "the brave" is singular is if it is directly modifying a singular noun that has been omitted but is clearly understood from context, or if it's used as a specific title/nickname (e.g., "James the Brave"). In standard everyday grammar, always treat it as plural.
