Knowing when to use **have** versus **has** comes down to the subject of your sentence. Both are forms of the verb "to have," but they pair with different pronouns and nouns.

---

## The Golden Rule

* Use **has** for **singular** subjects (he, she, it, or one person/thing).
* Use **have** for **plural** subjects (they, we, or multiple people/things) **AND** for the pronouns **I** and **you**.

### The Breakdown by Pronoun

| Pronoun | Verb | Examples |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **I** | **have** | I **have** a new backpack. |
| **You** | **have** | You **have** a great smile. |
| **He / She / It** | **has** | He **has** a dog. / She **has** a test today. / It **has** a scratch on it. |
| **We** | **have** | We **have** practice at 4:00 PM. |
| **They** | **have** | They **have** two cats. |

---

## Special Tricks & Common Tricky Situations

### 1. Look out for Names and Nouns

If you aren't using a pronoun (like *he* or *they*), look at how many people or things you are talking about.

* **One person/thing (Singular) $\rightarrow$ Has:** "Jessica **has** a guitar." / "The car **has** a flat tire."
* **More than one person/thing (Plural) $\rightarrow$ Have:** "Jessica and Marcus **have** a guitar." / "The cars **have** flat tires."

### 2. The Negative Trap (Don't / Doesn't)

When you make a sentence negative using words like *do not (don't)* or *does not (doesn't)*, the rule changes. You **always use have** after *don't* or *doesn't*, no matter what the subject is.

* *Correct:* He **doesn't have** his phone. (NOT: He doesn't has)
* *Correct:* She **doesn't have** time.

### 3. Asking Questions

Just like with negative sentences, when you ask a question starting with *Do* or *Does*, you **always use have**. The same goes for *Don't* or *Doesn't*.

* *Correct:* **Does** he **have** a pencil? (NOT: Does he has)
* *Correct:* **Do** they **have** the keys?
